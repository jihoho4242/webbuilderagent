# Premium Landing Page Skill

Use this skill to build a high-end single-page website for a consultant, studio, course, event, clinic, or premium service where the page must feel crafted and convert without looking generic.

## Inputs to Read First

- Product promise and primary buyer.
- Price point or perceived value tier.
- Primary conversion: book, buy, apply, contact, join waitlist.
- Existing brand constraints, proof, testimonials, and legal claim limits.
- Selected `DESIGN.md` or design-system direction.

## Good Patterns

- Hero makes a specific promise to a specific audience.
- Above the fold contains one CTA, one proof point, and one sensory brand cue.
- Sections alternate between emotional desire and rational proof.
- Copy uses concrete outcomes, timeframes, constraints, and objections.
- Visual details repeat: one border style, one accent, one image treatment, one CTA language pattern.

## Bad Patterns

- “Transform your business with innovative solutions” as hero copy.
- Six equal feature cards with generic icons.
- Fake social proof, unsourced numbers, or invented logos.
- Gradient backgrounds, glass cards, and sparkles with no brand reason.
- CTA overload: book/contact/download/join all competing in the first screen.

## Section Recipes

### 1. Premium Hero

**Goal:** establish value and next action within 5 seconds.

Required:

- Eyebrow: category + audience.
- Headline: result, not capability.
- Subheadline: mechanism and fit.
- CTA: one primary; optional understated secondary link.
- Proof: credential, client type, measurable outcome, or founder signal.
- Visual: editorial image, product/service artifact, or quote panel.

Quality bar:

- Headline fits in 2–4 lines on mobile.
- CTA remains visible above fold on 375px viewport.
- No critical claim appears without provenance status.

### 2. Problem / Stakes

**Goal:** make the visitor feel understood.

Structure:

- 3 pain points, each with real-world symptom and cost.
- One paragraph connecting pain to desired state.
- Avoid fear-mongering unless the domain requires urgency.

### 3. Offer Architecture

**Goal:** explain what is being bought.

Include:

- Service/product name.
- Who it is for and not for.
- Deliverables or experience stages.
- Timeline, format, and prerequisites if known.
- CTA after the offer summary.

### 4. Proof Stack

**Goal:** reduce perceived risk.

Use 2–4 of:

- Case study snapshot.
- Review/testimonial with permission status.
- Before/after metrics with source.
- Founder credentials.
- Process transparency.
- Guarantee or refund policy if real.

### 5. Process

**Goal:** show that the premium offer is structured.

Use exactly 3–5 steps. Each step needs:

- Verb-led title.
- What the visitor does.
- What the provider does.
- Tangible output.

### 6. Final CTA

**Goal:** invite action after proof.

Include:

- Restated outcome.
- CTA.
- Low-risk reassurance: response time, no-pressure consult, secure checkout, or calendar clarity.

## Component Recipes

### Premium CTA Block

- Background: dark ink or paper contrast.
- Include one human reassurance sentence under button.
- Do not place three buttons side-by-side.

### Testimonial Card

- Quote under 55 words.
- Attribution: name, role/context, permission/source state.
- Use plain typography, not quotation-mark decoration bigger than content.

### Pricing / Investment Panel

- Use “Investment” only if brand voice supports it.
- Include price or “starts at” when allowed.
- If price unavailable, explain the pricing basis and next step.

## QA Notes

- Check hero at 375x812, 768x1024, and 1440x900.
- Verify CTA text and link target are consistent across page.
- Confirm every testimonial/logo/metric has provenance or is marked draft.
- Run contrast checks for accent text over tinted backgrounds.
- Delete any section that repeats a previous claim without adding proof or decision help.

## Done Criteria

- The first screen answers: who it is for, what outcome, why credible, what to do next.
- Every section has a distinct job.
- Visual system uses declared tokens, not random one-off styles.
- No placeholder names, lorem ipsum, or fake proof remains.
